## 4. Phoenix Educational Wedges

### 4.1 What To Do After a Car Accident (Phoenix)

Car accidents are common in Phoenix due to highway traffic and daily commuting.

**Steps to Take**
1. Call 911 if anyone is hurt  
2. Move to a safe area if possible  
3. Get medical care, even if injuries feel minor  
4. Take photos of vehicles, plates, and the scene  
5. Exchange contact and insurance information  
6. Avoid discussing fault  
7. Save all paperwork  

**Phoenix Note**
Arizona follows a comparative fault system, meaning fault can be shared.

---

### 4.2 What To Do After a Truck Accident (Phoenix)

Truck accidents often cause serious injuries and involve commercial insurance.

**What Makes These Different**
- Commercial drivers and companies  
- Federal safety rules  
- More than one responsible party  

Many Phoenix truck accidents occur on I-10, I-17, and Loop 101.

---

### 4.3 What To Do After a Slip and Fall (Phoenix)

Slip and fall injuries can happen in stores, parking lots, or public spaces.

**What to Document**
- Photos of the hazard  
- Time and place  
- Witness names  
- Incident reports  

Extreme heat in Phoenix can increase pavement and property risks.

---

### 4.4 Evidence Checklist (Phoenix)

Gather:
- Photos and videos  
- Medical records  
- Police or incident reports  
- Insurance letters  
- Witness contact information

### 4.5 Arizona Injury Claim Timeline

Typical stages include:
- Immediate medical care  
- Insurance notification  
- Ongoing treatment  
- Claim review  

Arizona has strict deadlines. Missing them can limit options.

### 4.6 When To Call an Attorney (Phoenix)

You may consider speaking with a lawyer if:
- Injuries are serious  
- Fault is disputed  
- Insurance denies coverage  
- Long-term care is needed
